The Cartier fitting, done right

Sunglasses carry more weight than people expect — literally, on the bridge and the temples, and figuratively, as a piece you wear in public. A good fitting starts with proportion. The frame should sit level on the brow, rest cleanly on the nose, and stay put when you look down or turn your head. We adjust temple curl, nose-pad angle, and pantoscopic tilt in-house, and we refuse to let a piece leave looking almost-right.

Lens decisions, clearly explained

The lens conversation matters as much as the frame. Polarized lenses cut glare from roads and water but change how some screens read. Gradient lenses feel softer and more elegant in certain environments. Photochromic sun lenses shift with light. When you are deciding between options on a Cartier frame, we will lay out what each lens actually does, where it shines, and where it does not — then leave the decision with you. Prescription sunglasses are fully available, including progressive sun, polarized Rx, and high-index.

Prescription Cartier sun

Most Cartier sunglass silhouettes accept prescription lenses, including progressives. The conversation is worth having up front: some frames handle strong prescriptions more gracefully than others, and the right combination of frame geometry and lens index keeps a sunglass looking like a sunglass rather than a prescription piece that happens to be tinted. We guide that choice rather than rush past it.

Care across the seasons

Sun frames live a harder life than optical. Salt, sweat, sunscreen, and the occasional counter-edge take a toll. We adjust, tighten, replace nose pads, and swap in new screws as needed — usually while you wait. Cartier sunglass care is part of the relationship, not a charge sheet. Frames you bought elsewhere are welcome at our bench as well.

A Cartier sunglass is a piece you will reach for every morning. It should feel like it was made for your face, because that is what a real fitting delivers.
