What a Cartier fitting looks like

When you come in for a Cartier optical fitting, we take the piece through its proper paces. We try the silhouette against your proportion, compare it in different light, and study how it sits across the brow. The nose-pad, temple length, and pantoscopic tilt are all adjusted in-house. We will not rush you, and we will not pretend something is right when it is not. If a different Cartier frame — or a different designer — is the better answer, we will say so.

Prescription lenses, done properly

Cartier frames are fully prescription-ready. We work with single-vision, progressive, high-index, polarized, and specialty photochromic and blue-light lenses. Our lens counsel is plainspoken — we will explain the trade-offs, recommend what suits your prescription and your day, and leave the decision with you. For complex prescriptions, we take the time to measure properly and to explain what a given lens can and cannot do in a given frame.

Care that outlasts the season

Cartier eyewear is made to be worn for years. We treat it accordingly. Nose-pad replacements, hinge adjustments, temple-tip work, and screw replacements are part of the relationship — stop in for a quick adjustment whenever you need one. Frames you bought elsewhere are welcome as well; the care is the point.

Who comes to us for Cartier

Many come from Old Bridge itself; many more drive in from Marlboro, Matawan, Manalapan, East Brunswick, and other towns across Middlesex and Monmouth Counties. A thirty-minute drive for the right frame is a fair trade.

A Cartier frame deserves a fitting, not a transaction. That is the whole of our practice.
