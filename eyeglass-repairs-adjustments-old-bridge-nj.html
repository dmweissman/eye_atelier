Repairs & Adjustments · Old Bridge, NJ

Eyeglass repairs & adjustments in Old Bridge, NJ.

Nose pads, hinges, screws, temples, bridge realignments — boutique-level care for designer and everyday frames. Often while you wait.

Glasses live on a face, which means they take a beating. Temples loosen. Nose pads yellow. Screws back out. Bridges go slightly out of true after a drop. None of that needs to be the end of the frame — and none of it requires a full replacement. At Eye Atelier we handle eyeglass repairs and adjustments at our boutique in Old Bridge, NJ, often while you wait.

Clients from across Middlesex and Monmouth Counties bring us frames that deserve another few years of good service: Cartier, Dior, Gucci, Fendi, Balmain, Loewe, Dita, Oliver Peoples, Maui Jim, Chanel, along with everyday frames that have held up longer than expected. Luxury frames especially benefit from careful adjustment work.

What we do at the bench

  • Nose-pad replacements — standard, comfort silicone, or titanium stems as needed.
  • Hinge work — tightening, re-pinning, spring replacement where possible.
  • Screw replacements and re-threading for stripped holes.
  • Temple length, curl, and pressure adjustments to eliminate slipping or pinching.
  • Pantoscopic tilt and face-form adjustments for progressive wearers.
  • Bridge realignment after drops or accidental bending.
  • Frame cleaning and ultrasonic servicing for frames that have been through a lot.

Frames purchased elsewhere

Yes — we work on frames you bought somewhere else, especially for regular clients. Some repairs requiring manufacturer-specific hinges or parts may be limited to frames we stock, but most work — nose pads, screws, temple adjustments, bridge fit — is universal.

Lens overhauls

If the frame is in good shape but the lenses are scratched or the prescription is out of date, a lens overhaul is usually the right answer. We will remove the existing lenses, inspect the frame, take fresh measurements, and cut new lenses in-house — single vision, progressive, high-index, polarized, photochromic, or blue-light, as suits.

How long it takes

Most common adjustments are immediate. Nose-pad replacements, screw replacements, and temple tune-ups are five to ten minutes. Lens overhauls are typically a few days depending on the prescription and lens type. Hinge replacements requiring ordered parts can take a few days as well; we will give you a realistic timeline when you come in.

A pair of glasses is a tool. It should be kept in tune.
