Eye Exams · Old Bridge, NJ

Comprehensive eye exams in Old Bridge, NJ.

A proper exam, a patient conversation about your vision, and — if you are ready — a designer eyewear fitting in the same visit.

A good eye exam is about more than a prescription. It is a thirty-to-forty-five minute conversation about how you use your eyes — at work, on the road, at a screen, in low light — paired with the clinical measurements that turn that conversation into a correction. At Eye Atelier we offer comprehensive eye exams at our Old Bridge, NJ boutique, and we make it easy to pair an exam with a fitting in a single visit.

Clients come to us from across Middlesex and Monmouth Counties — Marlboro, Matawan, Manalapan, East Brunswick, Morganville, Holmdel, Aberdeen, Sayreville, Spotswood, Monroe — because a single considered visit for an exam, fitting, and lens cut is a better use of time than three separate trips.

What to expect at your exam

We take time for the conversation. Your vision history, your screen and driving habits, any eye fatigue or headache patterns, and what you want out of your next pair of glasses. The clinical portion follows standard comprehensive-exam protocol — refraction, visual acuity, ocular health — handled calmly and thoroughly.

Exam plus fitting

If you came in for both, we move directly from the exam to the frames. Your prescription is fresh, the pupillary distance is captured, and you can walk straight to the tray without leaving the boutique. For progressive wearers this is especially valuable — the measurements for a progressive lens benefit from being taken at the frame, in your chosen silhouette.

Insurance and plans

We work with a range of vision insurance plans. Call (732) 696·8933 with your plan details before your visit and we will confirm coverage specifics so there are no surprises at the counter.

Kids' exams

For very young children, we recommend starting with a pediatric eye specialist; we fit once the prescription is in hand. For tweens and teens, our standard exam works well, and we pair it with a patient fitting through Les Petits, our kid-scaled corner.

An exam and a fitting in one considered visit. That is the relationship we are trying to build.

How often should I have my eyes examined?

Every one to two years for most healthy adults without prescription changes. Children, older adults, and patients with a known eye condition should follow their doctor's guidance.

How long does an exam take?

Typically thirty to forty-five minutes. Add time if you plan a fitting afterward.
